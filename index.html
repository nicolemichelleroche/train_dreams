23 1/2 Train Dreams

A Meditation

by Nicole Roché


Now that I’m home all the time, here at 23 1/2 Walnut Street in Potsdam, New York, I have recommitted myself to documenting the trains that pass through my yard each day. This entails recording the time of day, direction (north- or south-bound), duration, notable contents, notable graffiti, and, occasionally autobiographical notes from my day.

This might sound tedious, obnoxious, or crazy.

You’re either the kind of person who wants to keep a detailed record of a train schedule, or you’re not.

You’re either the kind of person who wants to read another’s person’s train schedule, or you’re not.




Methodology


Notes on the Train Schedule / How to Read the Train Schedule



Week 1 (March 28–April 3)— I didn’t know what I was looking for yet. Missing or incomplete data. Most importantly, I wasn’t writing down the engine numbers yet. However, this first week was important for helping me to define my data set and because it helps establish the existence of particular patterns, such as time or day or daily frequency.

Week 2 (April 4–10)— Much improved. Still not writing down the engine numbers. We’ll get there.

Week 3 (April 11–17)— I am finally developing eyes for this project. A standardized data set emerged this week with the addition of the engine numbers. This was the first time I notice the Tuesday/Thursday “baby train” schedule (#6531).
